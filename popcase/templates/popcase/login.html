Population Cancer Assessment and Surveillance Engine (PopCASE)

Population cancer research • Catchment area surveillance • Standardized linkage

PopCASE is an informatics tool to facilitate population cancer research and catchment area surveillance. It enables cancer centers and organizations running cancer registries to link patient-level registry data to community and health system data sources using a standardized data model based on the patient’s residential address at diagnosis.

  • U.S. Census 5-year American Community Survey (ACS) — community demographics, socioeconomics, housing, transportation, and other social drivers of health
  • CDC PLACES — cancer screening, behavioral risk factor, and healthcare access measures
  • CMS Provider Directory — healthcare provider (specialty) and facility locations
  • FDA Mammography Facility Registry — U.S. mammography facility locations
  • HRSA Health Professional Shortage Areas (HPSA) — counties, subcounty divisions, and census tracts designated as HPSAs
  • Database of Redlined Neighborhoods — census tracts with historical redlining designations

  • Age-adjusted incidence, mortality, and other outcomes contextualized with community and health services access data
  • Outputs stratifiable by demographics, cancer characteristics, or geography (county, municipality, zip code, census tract)
  • Geographically stratified incidence and mortality with corresponding U.S. figures for comparison
